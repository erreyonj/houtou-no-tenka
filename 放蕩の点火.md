## Act 0 - The Official Version:

Four unknown officials sit in the formal reception hall of the district building in the capital of Sansamor.
A captain of the Vide family detail sits across from the Sansamor party kneeling, head bowed. 
The officials lean back and forth speaking to one another in hushed tones until a door opens from the side of the room.

*thunk... thunk... thunk... thunk*

Heavy boot footsteps sound as a fifth figure enters the room and it's as if the air pressure gets caught in a vacuum. The conversations simultaneously cease and the officials noticeably straighten their posture. The Captain glances, recognizes the figure and bows significantly lower. 

Captain (internal): *Wait-- wait is HE doing here? The circumstances of all of this are incredibly strange, but... I thought this was a standard hearing at the request of Lord Blanc... that's--*

The figure sits behind the officials, centered in the room. His presence looms and permeates the space in an overwhelmingly endearing, yet unsettling way. This is the King of Sansamor.

Official #1: "At your word, Souverain."

This internal inquiry is to initiate the investigation into the infiltration of Vide Manor and of the tampering of the safe known as 'Lé Identifique' by the criminal known as Kuramoto Maseru. It's been reported that you were the captain on duty at the time of these events. Is that correct?"

Hidetada: "That's correct, sir."

Official #2: "Then you would understand why we seek to rule out all possibilities. Cooperation, coercion and the like?"

Official #1: "Let's end the discussion here. We thank you for your time Captain Hidetada. From your report we will reconstruct the events of the Lé Identifique incident."

Hidetada (slightly confused): "Wait-- that's it?"

Official #1: "Yes, thank you Captain."

Hidetada: "But my lords, the details of the artifact. We have more to report. And Maseru he--"

Official #1: "As I stated Captain. That will be all."

Hidetada: "Sir, if I may?"

The third official booms.

Official #3: "No Captain, you may not! You will have access to the published--"

King Takahito has risen from his seat and places a hand on the third Official's shoulder and looks to the captain with a warm expression. The official is immediately silent.

Official #1: "Souverain?"

King Takahito: "Speak freely, Captain. What more do you have?"

Hidetada: "...Thank you, Souverain... All I have is a question: Would all of this have anything to do with the blockade of Bazaar Galash?"

All of the officials laser their focus on the Captain.

Official #1 (hints of surprise and diversion): "...What blockade?"

## Act I - Without Love:

### Scene I

A young Maseru Kuramoto leans against a post outside a large tent setup on the beaches of Bazaar Galash. We see ranks of soliders moving to and from, setting up housing and barracks. Maseru watches them intently. The Sansamor-born soldiers act with an alarming normality. He even sees a few of the newer recruits *smiling*. He watches lines of Bazaarians begin to form at different tents. Merchants, fisherman, seamstresses. The confusion on their faces. A stark contrast to the utter indifference of the soldiers and visiting families from Sansamor. Then he hears his name being called.

Lieutenant Ketsuteru: "Kuramoto!"

He enters the tent.

Maseru: "Yes Lieutenant. You know, you're not in a dungeon. I can hear you just fine."

The Lieutenant is unbothered by him.

Ketsuteru: "Kuramoto, we simply don't have time for your antics. You were the sole jutting cuticle in our formation this morning. Your record, while impressive, will not preclude us from pulling you slowly and painfully from from the hand of our Souverain."

As the Lieutenant gathers his belongings he walks past Maseru toward the tent opening.

Ketsuteru: "We still have encampments to erect, markets to formalize, leaders to reform. We will have no blemishes."

Maseru: "Stomach turning visual accomplished, sir."

Ketsuteru: "I'm glad you understand my gravity, mon garçon! Rejoin your unit this evening. The 50th will be instrumental in motivating the Perma-Ice harvesting companies to make some extra trips this season."

Maseru (internal): *Wonderful, I didn't know we could squeeze blood from ice*

Ketsuteru: "And if you happen across a recruit Rodolfo Miria, please take him with you. He's easily influenced, and you being his example... brings me joy."

Maseru: "At your word, Lieutenant."

As the tent opening closes behind the Lieutenant, Maseru sucks his teeth, then spits at the ground.

Maseru (internal): *But I'm not your "boy".*

### Scene II

As dusk approaches, Maseru heads out of the tent and walks further inland from the beach. 
The lines have only gotten longer. He sucks his teeth again. 
As he approaches the edge of the barracks, he passes his unit's tent and heads towards the village market. 
A soldier standing outside the 50th's tent notices him and looks puzzled. 

Cascading voices and buzzing strings making music start to fill the night as Maseru browses the market. 
He discards the torso of his uniform nearby and unties his hair. 
Just as he is plotting his path for the evening, the soldier from earlier suddenly appears next to him.

Rodolfo Miria: "Oh, you must stand too close to the canons on your left side. You didn't hear me at all."

Maseru: "Good Lord, where did you come from? ...You must be Miria?"

Miria: "Rodolfo Miria, at your word!"

Maseru: "Yea, at ease. Ease it all the way. You don't need to do any of that here. Let's just blend in... Well I will at least."

Miria: "Understood!"

The two begin to walk and talk as Maseru moves through the crowd.
Miria keeps up, but barely.

Miria: "Honestly, I'm just glad I get to work with you. I heard you embarrased the Sansamor recruits at your coronation trials. Recruits been talking about that for years."

Maseru (smirking): "If you say so kid..."

Amused by his own highlights being brought back to him, Maseru stops suddenly and pulls Miria behind him around a draped fabric between two stores.

Maseru: "...hold up."

On the other side, two Sansamor soldiers converse with a merchant.

Soldier 1 (overheard, clearly drunk): "...hold on, I've got my-- I've got 30vec here somewhere."

Maseru (whispering to Miria): "Do you even know why you're here?"

Maseru with knife drawn, kneels down and carefully saws at a pouch strung from the 1st Soldier's hip.

*sff... sff... sff... sff...*

Soldier 2 (overheard): "...they haven't been given their money yet..."

Soldier 2: "No, he's not gonna take it you idiot! Just give him what you grabbed earlier."

Soldier 1: "Damn it, its gone!"

The soldiers become frustrated and walk off.

Maseru: "Why you're *really* here?"

Miria: "I mean I'm just here to make money to send back to matant mwen. Sans gave me an opportunity. My family's business would be set for generations with what I'd make."

Maseru shakes his head as the two approach a small tea and wine house.

Miria notices the pain in his eyes. 

Maseru: "That's cute about your aunt I guess. But *You're* here because there's something here **they** want. Something they want bad enough to move fleets for. I don't know for what yet, but they do."

Maseru: "But, me? *I'm* here, for *this*."

As they look out a man wearing heavy boots and fur sits down nearby in the tea house.
Maseru notices him peripherally, but continues to talk to Miria.

Maseru: "I'm here for this... before no one is..."

Another beat.

Maseru: "So, let's celebrate! Another round on me, Miria."

The two cheers and the night pours in.

Suddenly Maseru wakes to Miria kicking his boot as he lies on ground outside the 50th's tent. 
His unit is standing in formation in front of him while Lieutenant Ketsuteru gives the days orders.

Maseru (half groan/half whisper): "What... the hell happened?"

Miria: "Well after you talked more about Bazaarian culture, you got blackout drunk and started standing on tables and singing. I went to go get our uniforms and by the time I got back you were gone. Apparently some patron named Atamoro brought you back here... Sang alot with him."

Maseru: "Wi, Lieutenant. Miria and I found a harvester named Atamoro."

Lieutenant Ketsuteru: "Yes... of course *you* did. The 50th will resume assimilation activities in the market, wharf, and monastery. Then, find this Atamoro and have him charter an additional northern journey. Advise him he is to return with no less than 120lbs of Perma-Ice."

The majority of the soldiers share confused looks and begin to murmur. 
Maseru struggles to hold his anger and glares directly at the Lieutenant.

Lieutenant Ketsuteru: "You have your orders. Procéder. Sans plus."

The energy today is already a far cry from the vibrance of the prior evening. Soldiers now outnumber villagers close to 3:1. As the pair travel with their unit, Miria begins questioning Maseru on the confusing order they received.

Miria: "Kuramoto, what kind of order was that? Don't individual harvesters typically return with 40 or 50lbs at most?"

Maseru ignores him, far too preoccupied with unnerving synchronization of all of the various units and their leaders. They watch as squad leaders run "safety inspections", section off entire plots of beach and fishing paths so that the famililies of high-ranking officers can have grazing room with views.

Miria: "Kuramoto?"

Maseru: "Stop calling me that. If you're gonna be with me, mwen rele Maseru. And if you don't know why we got that order, then you weren't paying attention last night."

Maseru breaks off from the unit and heads toward the store owner from last night.
Miria follows, but remains a few steps behind now. Maseru notices a squad leader from a different unit conducting a currency exchange and sees that the store owner appears to have made off nicely by converting the pouch Maseru returned the night before. While lingering there, Maseru catches the eye of the merchant who appears elated at first, but whose expression turns sour once he realizes that he recognized Maseru watching from afar and that the man who gifted the pouch was in fact one of these same exploiters. 

Maseru departs dejected, but hides it well. The two wander a bit further and encounter a few more soldiers from different units. These appear to be off duty and Sansamoran. Their children run around them wearing items entirely too big laughing and giggling. The soliders notice Maseru and flag the pair down.

## Act II - Complicity:

### Scene I

After getting directions, the pair embark. 
The temperature has already shifted noticeably cooler just north of the beaches.
The late afternoon sun offering little to make a difference.
During the journey, the silence between the pair grows with each moment.
As they arrive at the foot of the stairs leading to a grand monastery, Miria breaks the silence.

Miria: "So you mentioned you have a daughter..."

A flash of Maseru's daughter waving to him from a Sansamor capital building window.
Maseru waits a moment before responding.

Maseru: "Wi, epi?"

Miria: "So what's she like? How old--"

Maseru: "Look, frè mwen..." 

Maseru sees through the attempted connection and skips to the meat of the conversation. 

Maseru: "Nitahnia is home and I love her. But, I didn't even find out I was from there until I was 19 years old. Mi papa... him adopt me... into Sans."

As the pair ascend jagged stairways up to the Monastery, they don't break conversation.
Maseru remembers moments of his childhood during the climb.

[VO] Maseru: *"Back then, my nèg yo... Makoto, Tetsuya. They from the Mother. In Sans, all of us had nothing, but them... They had something."*

The Three young boys meet for the first time, Makoto and Tetsuya stand stolid but recognize something kindred in Maseru even if they can't place it.

In in the present, Miria and Maseru crest a spiral section of the stairs.

[VO] Maseru: *"Them have roots, while mine were pulled up."*

Memories from somewhere unknown show:
*- A Sansamor soldier walking away from a Nitahnian woman in a doorway, baby in arms.*
*- Maseru being presented to a Sansamor official for naming by his adoptive father.*
*- Maseru as a young child in Sansamor dress.*

Maseru: "I guess it seems that way, huh?"

Maseru reaches out a hand at the top step and pulls Miria up.

Maseru: "I know some of you look up to me... but, you gotta know I'm in this thing. Like a part, ya kno? And I like you..." 

Somehow Maseru manages lethality and brotherly love in the same breath.

Maseru: "...But you talk a lot and if you mention my daughter in front of, or to the wrong people... I'll kill you."

Miria glances down, not sulking but processing.
Maseru takes a few paces before turning back. 

Maseru: "I can't allow her to be used to make me do anything worse than what I've already done."

He pauses.
The two men now stand on either side of the monastery gate.

Miria (chuckling): "Look man... It's not like I see you as apa pou Bondye or something..."

A beat.

Miria: "You know why I'm doing this: My Aunt... I get it. When it comes to you.. I just... see what I see."

The two lock eyes.
Maseru acknowledges him.

Miria: "I don't need to know anything about your daughter... But I can go make sure those two actually get her something. Take the time you need."

Maseru nods and the two part ways.

After years of time and multiple deployments, he's still only vaguely familiar with what Bazaarians believe.
He's far more familiar with their cooking practices and rum refining techniques.
He enters the hall, trying to remain open. 

Inside the main corridor, there are rows of tables around 1-1.5ft high.
A few sparse students sit relaxed at the tables, but nose deep in parchment.
He walks through the corridor while moonbeams slice through the draped sail canopy above.
Luscious tapestries with rounded fractal-like designs hang from a curved bamboo gridshell. 
At some tables an unknown insignia is labeled with the Bazaarian deity's name: "Galash".

Further in, there are a few raised nooks underneath gaps in the sails.
Maseru takes a seat at one of them.
He shifts his weight, not used to the confined space. Even the barracks he could stand in.
Kneeling or sitting was required here.

Maseru (internal): *"...Um... I'm not sure why I'm here... This time, I mean."*

Maseru (internal): *"I'm not sure who I'm talking to either. Bazaarians have a way of thinking. And that's what they call you..."*

### Scene III

- All others creating an arc above

### Scene IV 

The night is still and black. A low rumble and whooshing begins to grow.
Then the sound of metal carving ice and many feet pounding over a frozen solid ground begins to thunder.

*krrshhhhhhhh.... krrshhhhhhhh*

*Pant... pant... pant... pant...*

The breath of a team of sled dogs slices through the wind. Everyone elese is holding their breath.
Trying to hold on to whatever warmth they can muster.

That sound of panting continues as it bleeds into earlier.
Maseru is kneeling at an outdoor kennel not far from the monastery where Atamoro's dogs play before the journey.
Three men are packing the last of their supplies next to Maseru.
They load bonded metallic frames (around 92 x 60cm) and tether onto the sleds.
As he plays with the dogs he looks up to his crewmates looking a little puzzled.

Maseru: "Hey... any of you know what they hell a Kermode is?"

They offer little more than condescending head shaking.

Genji (muttering under breath): "...sounds like bear food."

Another team member, Daichi stands nearby organizing tools.
He has a thin, rhombus like scar covering his face over his nose and underneath his eyes.

Daichi: "Kusho, even my little boy knows what a Kermode is!"

Maseru frowns and returns to the dogs.
Atamoro and another man look on from inside through a window.

Atamoro (jokingly): "I thought you said your people were suffering at the hands of Sansamor, Shigenobu. This one seems fine."

Shigenobu Kanemori: "After all of our trips together... you should know I'm unbothered by Nitahnians who choose life with Sans. They go their way and I go mine. With these hands, I gave everything I had to secure pwosperite for Nitahnia. People like him still make their own choices."

Atamoro: "Good! Let's go meet him... he'll be travelling with you."

Shigenobu sighs.

Atamoro: Maseru! One other person you have to meet. This is Shigenobu Kanemori. He's the standard when it comes to Nitahnian safemaking and metallurgy. A true artist! He invented the stabilization plating for my sled runners."

Shigenobu waves off the compliments and walks up to Maseru, subtly sizing him up.

Shigenobu: "I'm just a man who seeks to master himself and let nothing master him. It's that mastery that makes this expedition just stupid and not suicide."

Shigenobu's eyes narrow to Maseru, then a beat.

Shigenobu: "What have you mastered?"

Maseru: "Bon, mesye! I'll let you be the judge, but if I had to say... I've mastered the art of outsmarting misery."
